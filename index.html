Coffee Shops in Seattle

Best coffee shops around the city of Seattle

When speaking of coffee, the city of Seattle has much more than just Starbucks and Tullly's. Independent coffee shops line the streets around the emerald city and there are just so many of them to explore. The following are my top 10 favorites coffee shops in Seattle and they are perfect for either rainy gloomy days or a sunny summer day!

Slate Coffee Roasters

1309 NE 45th St, Seattle, WA 98105 Visit site »

The first time I had Slate's latte, I was surprised at how balanced the taste of milk and coffee can be. The espresso is done in the just right texture to achieve that Yin-Yang balanced sip for a morning on a rainy day!

Jenny's Recommendations: Slate's milk has a very special taste and I recommend any of their warm coffee with milk. Don't just order and go, sit down for a bit and enjoy the brick and mortar environment!

Mr. West Cafe Bar

720 Olive Way, Seattle, WA 98101 Visit site »

Stepping in Mr.West's Cafe Bar feels like stepping in a mini botanic garden. You are surrounded by different kinds of succulent plants and the white marble table goes nicely with the golden utensils. A cup of matcha tea latte along with a thick slice of avocado toast makes all the gloomy day go away!

Jenny's Recommendations: Awesome place to kill some time. Don't forget to order a runny poached egg with the avocado toast if you are both an egg lover and an avocado lover.

Anchorhead Coffee

CenturyLink Plaza, 1600 7th Ave #105, Seattle, WA 98101 Visit site »

Anchorhead Coffee is the PLACE to go for a hot summer day. Cold brew is good on its own or with milk mixed in. If you do order cold brew, pair it with the famous Quaffle(cinnamon roll waffle!) and double that pleasure on your tongue.

Jenny's Recommendations: Anchorhead has great pastries. It will satisfy your desire no matter you are into savory like me, or you have a sweet tooth. Do try their bacon cheddar chive scone if you are into savory!

MiiR Flagship

3400 Stone Way N, Seattle, WA 98103 Visit site »

If you are looking for a place to go on a rainy day, MiiR Flagship is a great choice! You can enjoy the emerald city style romance simply by sitting at the window seats and watch the rain drops. Cold brew or hot latte, anything is good here!

Jenny's Recommendations: MiiR Flagship is right around the corner of Gas Work park. Take a walk around Gas Work park after coffee is never a bad idea on a sunny Seattle day!

Elm Coffee Roasters

240 2nd Ave S #103, Seattle, WA 98104 Visit site »

If you are visiting downtown Seattle, try Elm's coffee if you have some time to stop for a break. White walls and wooden chairs makes the simplist but stylist place to have a cup of warm latte.

Jenny's Recommendations: Sit by the bar if you are interested in coffee. The baristas are friendly here and would love to chat with you about coffee.

Caffe Umbria

3531, 1201 Westlake Ave N, Seattle, WA 98109 Visit site »

If you happen to walk around south lake union, go to Caffe Umbria and have a simple espresso. This location is so close to the park and you can get up and enjoy the lake view anytime you want!

Jenny's Recommendations: I had one of the best prosciutto arugula panini sandwich here! Pair it with an iced tea and say hello to Summer!

Storyville Coffee

2128 Queen Anne Ave N, Seattle, WA 98109 Visit site »

This Storyville location is not as crowded as the Pike Place market one. Offers the high quality Storyville coffees and enjoy a quiet and nice afternoon here.

Jenny's Recommendations: This location is neat as it is lcoated in Queen Anne. There are so many nice and cute shops and boutiques around it and girls would love to walk around them after coffee.

La Marzocco Cafe

472 1st Ave N, Seattle, WA 98109 Visit site »

Into music and coffee? Then La Marzocco Cafe is the place for you! FM90.3 radio station is in the same building as the La Marzocco Cafe. Take a afternoon and explore the neighborhood around Seattle Center!

Jenny's Recommendations: Marscapone & Avocado toasts are awesome choices if you are looking for something to eat with your drink.

Cafe Presse

1117 12th Ave, Seattle, WA 98122 Visit site »

Unlike the other coffee shops, Cafe Presse is a French cafe and bar. You can get both great drinks and great coffee here. If you happen to be in the capital hill neighborho on weekends, come and try their brunch!

Jenny's Recommendations: If you are into egg and cheese just like I do, then you shouldn't miss out on their croque madame or baked eggs!

Cherry Street Public House

210 Occidental Ave S, Seattle, WA 98104 Visit site »

If you happen to be around the Union Square area in downtown Seattle, then go to Cherry Street Public House and try their bagles and coffee!

Jenny's Recommendations: Cherry Street Public House's homemade Quiche will make your mouth water everywhere because they are just simply a slice of heaven!
